NATIONAL SERVICE SCHEME

The National Service Scheme is a program of voluntary public service initiated by the Government of India under the Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports in the year 1969. It focuses on the development of the personality and character of students through community service and encouraging social responsibility, leadership, and national integration. Schools, colleges, and universities open this service for students. This program inspires the youth to participate in various social activities, including cleanliness, literacy camps, environmental conservation, health camps, and relief operation campaigns in disaster-hit areas. Through these activities, volunteers acquire life skills, a sense of team practice, and sense of civic responsibility. The motto of NSS is "Not Me, But You." This slogan reflects the spirit of selfless service and empathy toward the society. Thus, NSS is more than just service to the nation, as through its participation students also contribute towards their personal development and become good citizens and the leaders of this nation.


NSS Unit-Marian College Kuttikkanam(Autonomous)

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a scheme of the Government of India, Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports. It provides opportunity to students to take part in various community service activities & programmes. The sole aim of the NSS is to provide hands on experience to young students in delivering community service and nurture them to develop into responsible citizens. Marian NSS has rendered a host of community based services which involves sanitation drives, adoption of villages, tuition for school children, blood donation, service to inmates of hospices etc. Our volunteers rendered commendable relief work during the floods of 2018 and 2019. In February 2020, they successfully organized a National Integration Camp which had a pan Indian participation of NSS volunteers.

Activities

  1. Marian Buds: peer teaching to the students of plantation workers from Monday to Thursday
  2. Project Bulb: blood donation for hospitals and required people
  3. School adoption: Adopted Pallikkunnu school and renovated the school library
