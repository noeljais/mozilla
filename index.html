OVER VIEW

NSS (National Service Scheme) and NCC (National Cadet Corps) are both initiatives aimed at empowering youth in India, but with different focuses. NSS encourages students to engage in community service, helping them develop a sense of social responsibility by participating in activities like literacy programs, health drives, and environmental work. It emphasizes service to society and character building. On the other hand, NCC provides military-style training to students, fostering leadership, discipline, physical fitness, and patriotism. Through activities like drill, adventure training, and social service, NCC aims to shape responsible and patriotic citizens. While both programs aim to develop leadership and social awareness, NSS leans more toward community involvement, while NCC focuses on discipline and national integration.
